# Design Context — CineMatch

Synthesised from `docs/reference/cinematch/chats/chat1.md` (Claude Design session, 2026-05-04) and `CLAUDE.md`. Update this file when the brand or audience shifts.

## Users

A solo cinephile (the project owner). Logs ~110 films/year on Letterboxd, weighs film criticism, watches in the evening on phone or laptop. Uses the app to answer one question: *"What should I watch tonight?"* — without the noise of a generic recommender.

Mobile-first: primary device is iPhone, used at home (couch, bed, kitchen) in dim or low light. Desktop is secondary, used for browsing the back catalogue and reading taste analytics.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **observant, considered, cinephilic.**

- **Observant** — the app watches what you watch. Copy is descriptive, not prescriptive ("Slow, observational dramas with father-daughter intimacy" — not "You'll love this!").
- **Considered** — every recommendation has a stated reason. Numbers and bars are present but never dominant.
- **Cinephilic** — speaks the catalogue language: directors by surname in mono caps, runtimes in MIN, decade granularity. Comfortable referencing Sciamma, Kore-eda, slow cinema by name.

NOT witty, NOT excited, NOT promotional. The app is a quiet observer that happens to have good taste — closer to a Criterion booklet than a Netflix tile.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Editorial neo-noir.** Letterboxd-adjacent but more ambitious. Dark by default — the user is browsing at night and posters are the visual centerpiece, so a dark surface lets the colour of poster art carry.

- **Reference pole 1:** Letterboxd Pro (catalog density, mono metadata).
- **Reference pole 2:** Criterion Channel / a printed film journal (italic serif headlines, generous whitespace, restraint).
- **Anti-reference:** anything Netflix-like (auto-play hero video, "Top 10 in your country", oversized rounded chrome).
- **Theme:** dark. Light theme tokens exist for future use but are not the primary surface.
- **Single accent:** projector amber (`oklch(~0.72 0.17 60)`). Used sparingly for the *one* moment that matters per screen — the match score on a recommendation, the active tab, the focused field. Never decoratively.
- **Posters are the colour.** The chrome stays neutral so the films can be loud.

## Design Principles

1. **Posters carry the page.** Chrome recedes; the visual weight goes to film art (currently striped placeholders, real TMDB images in Phase 2+).
2. **One accent, used like punctuation.** Amber appears for ≤10% of pixels per screen, always tied to attention or status.
3. **Three voices of type, with discipline.** Instrument Serif italic for display moments (titles, tonight's pick), Inter for body and UI, JetBrains Mono for catalogue metadata (years, durations, percentages, eyebrows). Never mix the roles.
4. **Editorial spacing, not app-grid spacing.** Generous gaps between sections; metadata rows can be tight. Vary it — don't apply the same padding everywhere.
5. **Copy describes, doesn't sell.** "Why you'll love it" is acceptable. "Don't miss it!" is not.

## Constraints

- Vanilla JS, no framework, no bundler. Polish must work within ES modules + CSS.
- Tokens in `tokens.css` are the contract; do not introduce new colour or spacing values without adding them to the token system.
- Mobile-first: 390×844 reference viewport. Desktop = same layout, max-width 480px, centred.
- WCAG AA minimum on text contrast.
- React prototype at `docs/reference/cinematch/project/` is the visual ground truth — port faithfully, don't reinterpret.
